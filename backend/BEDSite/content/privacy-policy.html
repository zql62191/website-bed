What is binge eating disorder (BED)?

Binge eating disorder, now a distinct diagnosis in DSM-5®, is characterized by1:

  • Recurring episodes (at least
    once weekly for 3 months) of consuming an abnormally large amount of food in a short period of time (eg, within 2 hours), compared with others
  • Experiencing a lack of control
    over eating during an episode
  • Significant distress regarding binge eating

During a binge, adults with BED may consume food faster than normal, when not hungry, until uncomfortably full. They may also consume food alone due to embarrassment, and may feel disgusted, depressed, or guilty
after bingeing.

DSM-5 is a registered trademark of the American Psychiatric Association.

These are not the complete diagnostic criteria, but instead a brief summary. Diagnosis should be based upon a complete evaluation of the patient.
